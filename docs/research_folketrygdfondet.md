# Folketrygdfondet — synthesis for PhD interview (Tromsø / SFT)

> Briefing for second-round interview, May 2026. Sources cited at bottom.

## 1. What Folketrygdfondet is

Folketrygdfondet (FTF) is a Norwegian state-owned **special-statute company**
(*særlovselskap*) — its own legal entity (`§ 3` of Lov om Folketrygdfondet,
LOV-2007-06-29-44), owned 100 % by the state and reporting to the Ministry of
Finance. The Forvaltningslov (Public Administration Act) does *not* apply,
except for conflict-of-interest rules; all employees are bound by a strict
duty of confidentiality (`§ 6`). Org.nr. 971525061. The Riksrevisjonen and the
Ministry both supervise it; the board (seven members appointed by the Ministry,
plus two employee reps for admin matters) hires the CEO.

CEO since 2018: **Kjetil Houg**. Board chair: **Siri Birgit Teigum**. HQ in
Oslo (Haakon VIIs gate 2); new Tromsø office at Muségata 1. As of year-end
2025, FTF has **63 employees**, growing through the SFT build-out.

**Crucial distinction vs. the "oil fund":**
- **Statens pensjonsfond Norge (SPN)** — domestic/Nordic fund, managed by
  FTF. Equity + fixed income, mainly Norway. NOK 417 bn at YE 2025.
- **Statens pensjonsfond utland (SPU / "Oljefondet")** — managed by **NBIM**
  (Norges Bank Investment Management), *not* FTF. Don't confuse the two.
- **Statens fond i Tromsø (SFT)** — new (2024 law) Nordic small-cap fund,
  also managed by FTF, headquartered in Tromsø. NOK ~11.3 bn invested at YE
  2025; start capital NOK 15 bn (can scale to 30 bn).

The historical roots: FTF was established under the 1966 folketrygd law to
manage early surpluses; received deposits only 1968–1979; today the capital
is best understood as a deposit (*kapitalinnskudd*) from the state, with the
fund's accounting return added back to SPN.

## 2. The SPN mandate (Lovdata FOR-2010-12-21-1790)

Set by the Ministry of Finance. Key parameters relevant for the interview:

- **Strategic asset allocation**: **60 % equities / 40 % fixed income**
  (`§ 3-1`). Note: the encyclopaedia gives "around 60/40" — the formal mandate
  fixes the *index* weights at exactly 60/40 in the strategic benchmark, and
  net equity exposure must stay in the **50–70 %** range (`§ 3-6(4)`).
- **Geographic split inside each asset class**: **85 % Norway / 15 %
  Nordics-ex-Norway** (Sweden, Denmark, Finland; Iceland excluded for SPN).
  - Equity benchmark = **85 % OSEBX + 15 % VINX Benchmark** (dividend-adjusted
    for FTF's Norwegian tax position) — `§ 3-3`.
  - Bond benchmark = 85 % Bloomberg Barclays Global Aggregate w/ Norwegian
    issuers + 15 % Nordic-ex-Norway; the private-segment cap is 25 % HY
    (sub-investment-grade) — `§ 3-2`, `§ 3-6(3)`.
- **Tracking error (forventet relativ volatilitet)**: max **3 percentage
  points** ex-ante — `§ 3-6(1)`. This is the *active risk envelope* for SPN.
- **Single-name ownership cap**: 15 % of equity capital in any one Norwegian
  company; 5 % in Denmark/Finland/Sweden — `§ 3-6(7)`.
- **Responsible investment** (`Kapittel 2`): NUES, UN Global Compact, UNGP,
  G20/OECD principles; from 14 March 2025, an explicit long-term goal that
  portfolio companies align with global **net-zero emissions per the Paris
  Agreement** (§ 2-1 new). Climate risk measurement now required (§ 4-4).
- **GIPS**: return calculations follow Global Investment Performance Standards
  (§ 4-2).
- Exclusions/observations from SPU's ethical list flow through to SPN
  automatically.

## 3. The Tromsø fund (SFT) — what it is and isn't

SFT was created by **Lov om Statens fond i Tromsø (LOV-2024-06-25-67)** and
the **Mandate for SFT (FOR-2024-08-12-2096)**. Crucial points:

- **Benchmark: VINX Small Cap** (NI, dividend-adjusted), with two carve-outs:
  any company already in SPN's benchmark is removed, as are SPU-excluded
  securities. Resulting index ("SFTX") covers ~344 Nordic small-caps with
  ~NOK 1,500 bn free-float market value.
- **Country distribution** of the benchmark: Sweden ~50 %, Denmark ~22 %,
  Finland ~13 %, Norway ~9 %, Iceland ~6 %.
- **Sector tilt**: Industrials ~28 %, Health Care ~25 %, Financials ~21 %.
- **Tracking error cap: 5 pp** (vs 3 pp for SPN) — a wider active risk
  envelope, reflecting small-cap illiquidity.
- **Single-name cap: 5 %**.
- **Phasing rule (§ 7-5)**: while < NOK 5 bn is invested, the TE constraint
  doesn't bind. Threshold crossed **9 October 2025** — SFT is now a fully
  reporting GIPS composite.
- **No overlap with SPN** is allowed; cross-trades must be at arm's length.
- Investment director: integrated into FTF's equity department but based in
  Tromsø; first three "real" investments in June 2025; Q1 2026 NAV NOK 13.9
  bn, return –10.4 % (benchmark –10.6 %, so +20 bp differential).

Finance minister opened the Tromsø office in 2025; SFT is a deliberate
*regional-policy* instrument as well as a market-coverage extension — Houg
calls it "the bet on tomorrow's winners in smaller Nordic listed companies."

## 4. 2025 / Q1 2026 performance

**SPN (full year 2025)**
- Return **12.7 %** (NOK 47.4 bn result). Differential return **+76 bp**
  (gross), +69 bp net of costs.
- Equity portfolio +17.2 % (+80 bp vs benchmark); fixed-income +5.2 %
  (+70 bp).
- Since 2007 (current mandate): annualised differential return **+0.99 pp/yr**
  = NOK **69.4 bn** cumulative gross excess return (NOK 63.9 bn net), and
  +0.65 pp/yr since 1998. Faktorjustert alfa is statistically significant —
  active management has historically *improved* the risk/return trade-off,
  not just added beta.
- Cost ratio: ~7 bp (290 mn / ~420 bn).

**SPN (Q1 2026)**
- Return **+9.66 %**, benchmark **+10.28 %**, differential **−62 bp** YTD.
  Soft quarter for active equity (–105 bp on equity side; "kvalitetsselskaper"
  –108 bp). Bond portfolio flat to benchmark.
- Top equity holdings (Q1 2026): Equinor 13.9 %, DNB 8.9 %, Kongsberg
  Gruppen 6.6 %, Hydro 4.5 %, Aker BP 4.4 %.

**SFT (Q1 2026)**
- Return **−10.4 %**, benchmark **−10.6 %**, differential **+20 bp**.
- Top holdings: Jyske Bank, Avanza, Loomis, Sydbank, Mandatum, Huhtamäki,
  Kalmar, HIAB, Latour, Asker Healthcare.

## 6. Strategy 2026–2029 priorities

The new plan (published April 2026) extends 2022–2025 and emphasises three
pillars:

1. **Active management excess returns** across both SPN and SFT.
2. **Best practice in responsible investment & ownership** — climate risk,
   Paris alignment, voting/engagement.
3. **Strengthened technological capability** for investment process, risk
   management and reporting — explicit motivation for the PhD position
   and Tromsø build-out.

Houg's 2025 annual-report letter highlights five macro trends: ageing
demographics, geopolitical re-arming, climate-driven capex, **the AI
revolution** ("dominated by few capital-strong actors — bubble, or genuine
infrastructure return?"), and the end of 40 years of falling rates.

## 7. Bring to the interview

- Treat FTF as a **long-only, transparent, ESG-anchored** state investor —
  not a hedge-fund alpha-seeker. Models that rely on shorting or extreme
  leverage are off-mandate.
- Map your research to **the 5 pp TE envelope of SFT** (or 3 pp of SPN),
  not idealised market-neutral setups.
- Suggest concrete crossovers: LLM agents for company-meeting note synthesis
  (FTF held 677 dialogues in 2025), probabilistic factor models on the SFTX
  Nordic small-cap universe, scalable backtesting infra for the active
  equity team.
- Show you understand Tromsø is **strategic, not symbolic**: SFT is a
  regional-policy + market-coverage instrument; UiT has known strength in
  ML / probabilistic modelling / Visual Intelligence SFI.
